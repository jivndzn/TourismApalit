Municipality of Apalit

MUNICIPALITY

Apalit is a 1st class municipality in the province of Pampanga, Philippines. The municipality has a land area of 61.47 square kilometers and a population of 117,160 people as of the 2020 census. Apalit is home to several educational institutions, including: La Verdad Christian College, a private university founded in 1946.

The economy of Apalit is based on agriculture, manufacturing, and tourism. The town is known for its mangoes, rice, and poultry products. Apalit is also home to several manufacturing companies, including a textile factory and a furniture factory.


Apalit is a vibrant and historic town with a lot to offer visitors. Some of the town's most popular tourist attractions include:

  • The Apalit Catholic Church, which was built in 1582.
  • The Apalit Museum, which houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits on the history of the town.
  • The Apalit Rice Terraces, which are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • The Apalit Water Park, which is a popular swimming and water sports destination.
  • The Apalit Eco Park, which is a newly opened park with a variety of attractions, including a butterfly garden, a mini zoo, and a playground.
  • Apalit is also known for its delicious food, including its palitaw (a type of rice cake), sisig (a dish of chopped pork and vegetables), and pancit (a stir-fried noodle dish).


    • The town is also home to several festivals throughout the year, including:

  • The Apung Iru Fluvial Procession, which is a religious festival held every June 28-30.
  • The Apalit Mango Festival, which is held every April. The festival celebrates the town's mangoes, which are known for their sweetness and quality.
  • The Apalit Town Fiesta, which is held every September 29. The fiesta celebrates the town's patron saint, Saint Peter the Apostle.
